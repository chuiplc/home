Here are some of the information of the NBA championships from the past 5 years.


Golden State Warriors: 2014 - 2015

The Golden State Warriors won their third NBA championship title after forty years, since 1975. The “Most Valuable Player” was given to Stephen Curry, who only played in the NBA for six years at the time. Stephen Curry and Andre Iguodala led the way, both with 25 points each. Draymond Green scored in 16 points, 11 rebounds, and 10 assists, also known as a triple double (where a player gains double digits in three categories of the sport of basketball).




Cleveland Cavaliers: 2015 - 2016

One of the most incredible comebacks in NBA history. Beginning with a rough start, they were losing to a 3 to 1 lead of the Golden State Warriors in the finals. However, game by game, they started to catch up, in which eventually led up to a 3 to 3 tie. Avenging their loss from last year, they took the championship title. They not only became the first title for the Cleveland Cavaliers, but also broke the Cleveland Curse (a sports superstition claiming that the city of Cleveland centered on the failure to win a championship in any major league sport). They were the first NBA team to catch up in a finals 3 to 1 deficit.




Golden State Warriors: 2016 - 2017

After the trade of Kevin Durant from Oklahoma City Thunder to the Golden State Warriors. In the postseason, the team set a record of the most consecutive wins in the playoffs, which were 12 games in a row. They, again, matched up with the Cleveland Cavaliers in the finals. However, they dominated the team 4 - 1 in wins, and won the Golden State Warriors’ fourth championship title. This was also the first franchise win for Kevin Durant, who won the Finals “Most Valuable Player” as well.




Golden State Warriors: 2017 - 2018

Even though they did not win as many times as last year, the Golden State Warriors still managed it to make it through the playoffs and into the finals with the Cleveland Cavaliers once again. Though this time, they swept the Cavaliers 4 - 0, and had a repeat (winning the finals two times back to back), making it the 5th championship title of the Golden State Warriors. Kevin Durant was the NBA Finals MVP (Most Valuable Player) of the 2017 - 2018 season.




Toronto Raptors: 2018 - 2019

The most surprising win, and also one of the most happiest ones (especially for Canadians) in NBA history. Kawaii Leonard was traded from the San Antonio Spurs to the Toronto Raptors. Game by game, with strong determination, the team passed through the playoffs. One of the most iconic moments was the buzzer beater that Kawaii Leonard scored in the last seconds of the game against the Philadelphia 76ers. Into the finals, against the Golden State Warriors, they won 4 to 2, and became the first team to win a championship title that was outside of the United States. The Finals MVP (Most Valuable Player) went to Kawaii Leonard, as he was a key player in their games.
